01 Physical Prep60–90 min

The Why Buyer shows up → apartment + tower + you all confirm the dossier's story. Any single mismatch resets their trust model. The tower has to look as clean on arrival as the dossier says it is.
1.1
Desk area staging + cable cleanup
30 min
Clear everything from buyer's eye-line. The tower is the product, not the desk. Re-zip any loose visible runs (front I/O, GPU power, front panel). Wipe the desktop surface. This is the #1 trust-setter on arrival.
Not started — blocking Phase 02
1.2
Tempered glass panel clean (inside + outside)
10 min
Pull the glass panel, microfiber + isopropyl (70%+) on both sides. Smudges and dust are what buyers fixate on through glass. This single step disproportionately moves the "babied" claim.
Not started
1.3
Dust filter pull + rinse
10 min
Top, front, bottom filters → pull → rinse with cold water → air-dry while you do other steps → reinstall. Dusty filters visible through glass tank the magazine-ready read.
Not started
1.4
Fan blade + RGB trim wipe
10 min
Damp microfiber across all Aer RGB 2 blades + radiator fans. No compressed-air spray inside (it scatters dust onto RGB strips and the LCD — wipe, don't blow).
Not started
1.5
Final boot-clean check
10 min
Confirm the tower still boots clean with no GPU installed (UHD 770 headless). Log into the "Buyer" admin account you'll use at the meet. Validate CPU-Z / HWiNFO64 / CrystalDiskInfo open without errors. Don't skip this — a dirty boot at the meet kills the deal.
Not started
1.6
Print the dossier
5 min
Print elite-workstation-dossier-v15.html to PDF → print hard copy. Hand it to the buyer when they arrive. Physical doc = authority anchor. Even if they don't read it cover-to-cover, they hold it and mentally price it higher.
Not started

02 Evidence Capture30–45 min

The Bar Every major dossier claim needs a photo that backs it. If a buyer questions a claim, your reply is "see photo 4" — not a paragraph.
2.1
Tower photos (5–7 shots)
20 min
Shoot in daylight or with a single directional lamp — never overhead fluorescent. Required angles:
  • Hero: 3/4 front angle, RGB on, black background
  • Top-down: AIO + radiator + cable bar visible
  • Side profile through glass: all internals visible
  • Rear I/O: motherboard I/O + PSU cables
  • Cable management: back-panel zip-tied run
  • Z63 LCD close-up: showing the 2.36" display alive
  • Receipt stack: NZXT BLD + component receipts fanned out on desk
Blocked on 1.1

10 min
Phone vertical. Start with power button press → show POST → Windows login → desktop with CPU-Z already open showing 5.486 GHz boost. Demonstrates headless boot (no GPU) — worth $200 of trust on its own. Upload to unlisted YouTube, paste link in listing.

## Meet ProtocolWhen a qualified buyer shows

Default Flow Qualified buyer confirms → you propose QuikTrip Safe Exchange Zone OR offer inspection at your apartment (only if they provide ID + phone first) → meet, they inspect, they pay via Apple Pay or Zelle with instant confirmation visible on your phone, you hand over tower + printed dossier + receipt stack → done.
Bring to the meet
  • Tower + power cable (both IEC C13 and any peripherals you're including)
  • Printed dossier v15 PDF
  • Receipt binder (NZXT BLD + component receipts)
  • ItsWorthMore "Flawless" email (printed or phone-screen ready)
  • Your phone with Apple Pay + Zelle both open and signed in
  • Small monitor + HDMI cable if inspection requires boot (for QuikTrip meets)
  • Valid NC driver's license for ID exchange
Hard rules at the meet
  • Payment lands before the tower leaves your hands. Show the confirmation on your phone, then hand over.
  • No "I'll Zelle you when I get home" — ever.
  • No partial payment + "I'll pay the rest Friday" — ever.
  • No cash. Say it out loud if they pull it out: "Apple Pay or Zelle only, as the listing stated."
  • If they walk for any reason — let them walk. Don't negotiate down from firm at the table.

## Buyer ScreeningInbox management

The Filter Anyone who doesn't reference the dossier isn't qualified. If they ask a question already answered in the dossier, reply with the saved template. Don't retype the dossier in DMs.
Green lights
  • Asks specific question about the 13900K 0x12F microcode
  • Asks about the mobo warranty transfer
  • Asks if they can bring their own GPU for inspection boot
  • Asks for the Inside the Guts companion doc
  • Proposes SafeTrade meet without being prompted
These are builders and champions. Respond within the hour.
Red flags
  • Any offer below $1,700 — decline, don't counter. Saved template, move on.
  • "Can you ship it?" — no
  • "I can only meet at my house" — no
  • Asks if you accept PayPal G&S, Venmo, Cash App, crypto — decline
  • Generic "still available?" with no other context 48 hrs later with no reply to your template — mark as flake
  • Profile under 6 months old, no photos, no friends — flake

!! Risk RegisterPre-mortem

Dead inbox (first 72 hrs)
Mitigation: execute 4.4 crosspost multiplier. The dossier is stronger than the typical Marketplace listing — traction will come from multiple channels, not Facebook alone.
Lowball barrage
Mitigation: don't respond. Saved template "$2,000 firm — dossier has the math" and move on. Every minute spent negotiating with a $1,200 offer is a minute not responding to a qualified buyer.
Tech-savvy buyer challenges a claim at the meet
Most likely: the 6.37 TB vs 7 TB nameplate question. Your reply: "Nameplate is 7 TB (drives are advertised in decimal). Windows reports 6.37 in binary TiB. Same drives, same math every PC does." Move on.
Buyer backs out at the meet
Mitigation: don't negotiate down from firm in person. If they walk, they walk — the next qualified reply is already in the queue if Phase 04.4 is executed. The dossier is an asset; it doesn't expire.
