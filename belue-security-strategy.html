Property Assessment
The Belue Residence
Single-story brick-front, ~2,940 sq ft on approximately half an acre. Front-loaded driveway with attached garage, covered front entry, open backyard with rear access.

Homeowner Profile

Retired, practical, tech-curious, and privacy-conscious. He already uses a eufy camera outdoors for wildlife observation and appreciates the no-subscription model. A prior negative experience with Canary (billing and refund frustration) reinforces a strong preference for ownership-based systems with no recurring cost traps. He values quality and reliability but doesn't want gadget overload. Indoor cameras are acceptable in specific rooms but not assumed everywhere — privacy matters inside the home too.

Property Characteristics

Strengths: Single story simplifies camera mounting. Brick exterior provides solid mounting surfaces. Existing outdoor camera proves the homeowner is comfortable with installation. Half-acre lot gives generous buffer but requires wider-angle coverage at perimeter.

Key constraint: The right side of the house (facing from the street) receives limited sunlight due to tree coverage and orientation. Solar-only cameras are not viable on the right side. Any camera deployed there must be battery-powered with a charging plan, or hardwired.

Coverage Strategy
Six Zones, Four Priorities
Each zone is assessed by threat level, power availability, and recommended coverage type.
Essential

Zone 1 — Front Entry / Porch

Primary access point. Package delivery zone. Highest foot traffic from visitors and service providers. Requires clear facial capture and real-time alerts.

Power: Wired outlet accessible under eave or via doorbell wiring.

Essential

Zone 2 — Driveway / Garage

Vehicle monitoring, garage door visibility, and approach detection. Wide-angle coverage needed for full driveway sweep.

Power: Garage exterior outlet likely available. Solar viable on front-facing surfaces.

Essential

Zone 3 — Backyard / Rear Access

Least visible from street. Potential entry point via sliding doors, rear windows, or patio. Also the wildlife observation zone — existing eufy camera may cover part of this area.

Power: Exterior outlet likely. Solar may be viable depending on orientation.

Critical

Zone 4 — Right Side (Shaded)

Limited sunlight. Narrow passage between house and property line. Common blind spot for break-ins on single-story homes.

Power: Solar NOT viable. Must be hardwired. Floodlight camera is the ideal solution.

Recommended

Zone 5 — Left Side

Mirror of right side but with better sunlight potential. Lower priority if front and back are well covered. Consider if full perimeter closure is desired.

Power: Solar may be viable. Battery backup as alternative.

Optional

Zone 6 — Indoor (Selective)

Former positions: office, sunroom, front entry hall. Indoor cameras are privacy-sensitive — recommended only where the homeowner actively wants them.

Power: Plug-in. Easy to deploy and relocate.

System Design
Architecture: Two Strategic Paths
Both offer local storage and zero subscriptions. The choice depends on how permanent the installation should feel.

Wireless + HomeBase Hub

  • Battery or solar-powered cameras
  • Connect wirelessly to HomeBase S380
  • 16GB onboard + expandable storage (up to 16TB HDD)
  • Easy DIY install — no drilling for cables
  • Cameras repositionable anytime
  • Best for: most homeowners, flexible deployment

Wired NVR (PoE) System

  • Power-over-Ethernet cameras — one cable for power + data
  • NVR S4 Max central recorder with HDD
  • 24/7 continuous recording capability
  • No battery dependency, no solar needed
  • Professional-grade permanence
  • Best for: permanent installs, maximum reliability
Recommendation

For the Belue residence, the wireless + HomeBase architecture is the best starting point. It matches the homeowner's comfort level, is fully DIY-installable, and covers all critical zones without running cables. The NVR path is presented as an alternative for maximum permanence.

Installation Plan
Based on "The Right Move" package. Estimated total: 3–4 hours for two people. All DIY-friendly.

Unbox and Set Up HomeBase S380

Connect to router via Ethernet. Complete setup through eufy Security app. Add external HDD (2–4TB recommended) for expanded storage.

DIY · 15 min

Install Front Entry Camera (eufyCam S4)

Mount under porch eave at 7–8 feet height. Angle to capture faces approaching door and package delivery zone. Pair with HomeBase via app.

DIY · 20 min

Install Driveway Camera (eufyCam S4)

Mount on garage fascia facing driveway. Ensure solar panel faces south/southwest for maximum charging.

DIY · 20 min

Install Backyard Camera (eufyCam S4)

Mount on rear of house facing backyard and rear access. Covers sliding doors and patio. Supplements or replaces existing deer-watching camera.
