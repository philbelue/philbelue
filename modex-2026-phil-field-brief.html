Section C — 5 Fast Disqualification Questions

Ask in sequence. Know in 5 minutes whether the account is real IXON potential or noise. First question is the exec brief hard gate.

1. HARD GATE — US/CA manufacturing presence. "Where are your machines/systems manufactured — here in the US or at a parent facility abroad?"
→ US/CA = proceed. Imported = stop. This is the exec brief's hard qualifier.
2. Controls platform ownership. "On the machines or systems you ship, who picks the PLC — you standardize on Siemens/Rockwell/B&R, or is it customer-spec every project?"
→ Standardization = IXON fit. Customer-spec = they're not the buyer, the downstream integrator is.
3. Install base density. "How many systems or machines do you have deployed out there that you still support today?"
→ <50 = too early for fleet standardization. >200 = real scale pain.
4. Remote-service method today. "When a customer calls at 2am because a line is down, how does your team get eyes on the machine — truck roll, customer VPN, TeamViewer, or a platform?"
→ Truck roll / bespoke VPN = pure IXON pain. "A platform" = qualify which one, fast (overlap risk).
5. Service P&L posture. "Is remote service a revenue line for you today or a cost center?"
→ Revenue line = leadership is incented to invest. Cost center = budget battle.
